⚖️ What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple screening tool using weight and height to estimate body fat levels. It's widely used as a first-step health assessment — not a diagnostic tool.

Formula: BMI = weight(kg) ÷ height²(m)

📊 BMI Categories

  • Under 18.5: Underweight — consult a nutritionist
  • 18.5–24.9: Normal weight ✅
  • 25–29.9: Overweight — consider diet and exercise adjustments
  • 30–34.9: Obese Class I
  • 35+: Obese Class II/III — medical consultation recommended

⚠️ Limitations of BMI

BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. It may be inaccurate for: athletes (muscle weighs more than fat), elderly people (muscle loss), pregnant women, and children. Always consult your doctor for a full health assessment.
